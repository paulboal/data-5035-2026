# AI Data Center Site Selection: Midwest United States Strategic Analysis

## Role & Expertise

You are a senior infrastructure strategy consultant with deep expertise in:
- Large-scale data center development and operations
- Energy grid analysis and renewable integration
- Climate risk assessment and long-term resilience planning
- Regional economic development and regulatory environments

Your client is planning a 10-year, multi-billion dollar AI data center expansion across the Midwest United States and needs your expert recommendations.

---

## Objective

Identify and rank the **top 5 geographic regions** in the Midwest United States for large-scale AI data center development over the next decade (2025-2035).

---

## Evaluation Framework

Analyze each candidate region across these weighted categories:

### Category 1: Energy Infrastructure (High Priority)
- **Grid reliability**: Historical uptime, redundancy, and modernization investments
- **Power capacity**: Available megawatts and planned transmission expansion
- **Renewable energy trajectory**: Current mix, state mandates, and projected growth
- **Energy costs**: Current industrial rates and 10-year price stability outlook

### Category 2: Environmental Resilience (High Priority)
- **Water availability**: Sustainable supply for cooling (aquifer levels, municipal capacity)
- **Drought risk**: Historical patterns and climate model projections
- **Climate change exposure**: Temperature trends, extreme weather frequency
- **Natural hazard risk**: Seismic activity, flood plains, tornado corridors

### Category 3: Connectivity & Logistics (Medium Priority)
- **Fiber backbone proximity**: Distance to major internet exchange points and dark fiber routes
- **Transportation access**: Highways, airports, and freight corridors for equipment delivery

### Category 4: Business Environment (Medium Priority)
- **Tax incentives**: State and local programs for data center development
- **Labor market**: Technical workforce availability, educational institutions, cost of living
- **Regulatory landscape**: Permitting timelines, zoning flexibility, utility cooperation
- **Political stability**: Bipartisan support for industrial development

### Category 5: Community Factors (Lower Priority but Critical)
- **Public sentiment**: Historical reception of large industrial projects
- **Land availability**: Suitable parcels at scale with appropriate zoning
- **Community infrastructure**: Housing, services to support construction and operations workforce

---

## Analytical Approach

You are empowered to:

1. **Propose additional criteria** if you identify factors not listed above that materially impact site viability
2. **Adjust category weights** based on your analysis—explain your reasoning if you deviate from the suggested priorities
3. **Model scenarios** (e.g., aggressive renewable adoption, water scarcity acceleration, policy shifts)
4. **Identify emerging corridors** that may not be obvious today but show strategic promise

---

## Required Output

Structure your response as follows:

### 1. Executive Summary
A 2-3 paragraph synthesis of your top recommendation and key findings.

### 2. Ranked Recommendations
For each of your 5 recommended regions:
- **Region name and boundaries** (be specific: counties, metro areas, or corridors)
- **Overall score or tier** with brief justification
- **Top 3 strengths** for this location
- **Top 2 concerns** and mitigation strategies
- **Ideal use case** (e.g., hyperscale campus, distributed edge, GPU-intensive training)

### 3. Comparative Analysis
A summary table or matrix comparing all 5 regions across your evaluation categories.

### 4. Key Tradeoffs
Explicit discussion of the tensions between competing priorities (e.g., renewable energy vs. grid stability, tax incentives vs. long-term political risk).

### 5. Risks & Uncertainties
- Factors that could invalidate your recommendations
- Assumptions you are making that should be validated
- Black swan scenarios to monitor

### 6. Data Sources & Validation
List the specific data sources, reports, and databases you would consult to validate this analysis (e.g., EIA reports, USGS water data, state economic development agencies).

---

## Constraints

- **Do not use fixed scoring weights**—optimize holistically for long-term strategic viability
- **Prioritize political defensibility**—recommendations should withstand public scrutiny
- **Think in decades, not quarters**—short-term incentives should not override long-term resilience
- **Be specific**—name actual counties, utilities, and infrastructure where relevant
- **Acknowledge uncertainty**—clearly distinguish high-confidence assessments from speculative projections

---

## Begin Analysis

Proceed with your strategic assessment. Take a structured, methodical approach—work through each evaluation category systematically before synthesizing your final recommendations.
